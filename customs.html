CUSTOMS & TRADITIONS

Tagbanua People

Indigenous group known for rituals, nature worship, and their ancient script. Among the most culturally preserved groups in Palawan.

Palaw'an Tribe

Known for the Tarek ritual, nature-based practices, and deep respect for spirits of forests and mountains.

Batak Tribe

One of the oldest tribes in the Philippines, known for forest living, gathering, and shamanistic healing rituals.

Pagdiwata

A Tagbanua ritual ceremony done to give thanks, ask for blessings, or celebrate successful harvests.

Tarek Ritual

A Palaw’an tribal ceremony with dancing and chanting, believed to bring protection and good harvest.

Bayanihan

A tradition of unity and helping neighbors, especially during farming, building, and community activities.

LANGUAGES

Cuyonon

Former lingua franca of Palawan; widely used in history, culture, and traditional communities.

Tagbanua Language

Spoken by Tagbanua groups; one of the few remaining Philippine ethnolinguistic languages with its own script.

Agutaynen

Spoken in northern Palawan including Busuanga and Agutaya islands.

FOOD & CUISINE

Tamilok

A mangrove woodworm delicacy, slippery like oysters and eaten raw with vinegar.

Crocodile Sisig

A local specialty made from farm-raised crocodile meat, sautéed with spices.

Lato (Sea Grapes)

Fresh seaweed salad eaten with tomatoes and vinegar; popular in coastal communities.

Nido Soup

A luxurious soup made from edible bird’s nest, known as a delicacy in Taytay.

Kinilaw

Raw fish marinated in vinegar, onions, ginger, and spices; a coastal favorite.

Cashew Nuts

Palawan is one of the top cashew producers; sold roasted, glazed, or brittle.
