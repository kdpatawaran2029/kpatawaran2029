Baragatan Festival

Baragatan Festival is Palawan’s biggest annual celebration held every June in Puerto Princesa City. It brings together all the towns of Palawan for colorful parades, street dancing, food booths, and cultural shows that highlight the province’s traditions.

Most events take place at the Provincial Capitol Complex, making it a great spot for visitors to explore local crafts, try Palawan delicacies, and enjoy nightly performances. It’s one of the best times to experience Palawan’s culture in one place.

Balayong Festival

Balayong Festival is Puerto Princesa’s annual foundation celebration held every March, inspired by the blooming of the pink balayong (cherry blossom) trees. The festival features street dancing, cultural parades, and performances that symbolize renewal, growth, and the city’s vibrant spirit.

Most activities are held around the Puerto Princesa City Baywalk and major public plazas, where visitors can enjoy local food stalls, concerts, and the colorful Balayong-themed events. It’s one of the best times to see the city filled with pink decorations and lively cultural showcases.

Pasinggatan Festival

Pasinggatan Festival is Taytay’s annual celebration held every May, honoring the town’s rich heritage and its title as the “Kingdom of the North” of Palawan. The festival showcases vibrant street dances, cultural parades, and performances that highlight Taytay’s history, traditions, and strong community identity.

Most of the festivities take place around the Taytay Municipal Grounds and the iconic Fort Santa Isabel area, where visitors can enjoy food fairs, concerts, and exhibits featuring local products. It’s one of the best times to experience Taytay filled with lively colors, music, and cultural showcases that reflect its proud past.

Kulambo Festival

Kulambo Festival is celebrated in the municipality of Dumaran every June, symbolizing protection, resilience, and the local community’s appreciation for the simple mosquito net—an essential part of daily life in the area. The festival features creative street dances, parades, and performances where participants wear colorful kulambo-inspired costumes that highlight both tradition and resourcefulness

Most events are held at the Dumaran Municipal Plaza and nearby public spaces, offering visitors food stalls, cultural shows, and displays of handmade kulambo products. It’s a unique celebration that showcases how a humble household item can inspire artistry, unity, and pride within the community.
